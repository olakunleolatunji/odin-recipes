Amala

Description

Amala is a classic Yoruba swallow made from yam flour, known for its soft, stretchy texture and earthy flavor. It’s traditionally enjoyed with soups like ewedu, gbegiri, okra, or efo riro, making it a cultural staple across southwestern Nigeria.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 cups of Yam flour (Elubo)
  • 4 cups of Water (bring to a rolling boil)
  • As needed - Additional hot water (for adjusting texture)

Steps

  1. Bring water to a rolling boil in a large pot.
  2. Reduce heat to medium and quickly stir in yam flour while turning continuously to prevent lumps.
  3. Scrape down the sides of the pot, add a splash of water on top, cover, and let it steam for 5 minutes.
  4. Stir again vigorously, adding more hot water if needed, until the amala becomes smooth, stretchy, and lump‑free.
  5. Serve hot with your preferred soup — ewedu, gbegiri, okra, egusi, efo riro, or obe ata.

Note: Amala is commonly eaten with Ewedu, Gbegiri, or Abula, but it's food. Just eat it with anything you feel like!
