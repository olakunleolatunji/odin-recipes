Ewa Agoyin

Description

Ewa Agoyin is a smoky, deeply fried Yoruba pepper sauce served over extra‑soft mashed beans. It’s famous for its dark color, rich aroma, and perfect pairing with Agege bread, plantain, or assorted meats.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried crushed chilies/peppers
  • 1 white onion
  • 1 large red onion
  • 1 seasoning cube
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups of palm oil

Steps to Make Ewa Agoyin Sauce

  1. Slice half of the red onion; blend the other half with the white onion, dried chilies, and 1 cup of water.
  2. Heat palm oil for about 3 minutes — hot but not bleached.
  3. Add the sliced red onions and fry for 3 minutes.
  4. Pour in the blended pepper mixture and fry on medium heat for 15–25 minutes, stirring continuously.
  5. Allow the sauce to darken and become grainy like sand; add seasoning cube and salt.Allow the sauce to darken and become grainy like sand; add seasoning cube and salt.
  6. Fry for another 3–4 minutes until dry but not burnt.

Steps to cook the Beans

  1. Add beans to boiling water, simmer 5 minutes, turn off heat, rinse, and set aside.Add beans to boiling water, simmer 5 minutes, turn off heat, rinse, and set aside.
  2. Boil 5 cups of water and cook the beans until very soft, adding more water as needed.
  3. Add salt and mash lightly if desired.
  4. Serve hot with the Agoyin sauce and Agege bread.Serve hot with the Agoyin sauce and Agege bread.
