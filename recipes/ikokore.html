Ikokore

Description

Ikokore is a rich, comforting Ijebu water‑yam pottage cooked in a flavorful palm‑oil broth with assorted proteins. It’s cheap, filling, and perfect for those “month don pregnant” days when you need something hearty without breaking the bank.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 medium water yam
  • 16 small chunks of beef
  • 1 cup of smoked fish pieces
  • 1/2 cup smoked prawns (cleaned)
  • 1½ cooking spoons palm oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped effirin (scent leaf)
  • Dried pepper to taste
  • Seasoning to taste
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Peel, wash, and grate the water yam; add a little salt and mix.
  2. Season and steam the beef with onion, pepper, salt, and seasoning.
  3. Add smoked fish and prawns to the pot, adjust seasoning, add a bit more water, and cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Add palm oil and cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Using your hand, drop the grated yam in small balls into the pot, leaving space between them so the stock can bubble and cook them.
  6. Reduce heat to very low; allow the yam balls to cook without stirring. Shake the pot gently if needed. Cook until balls turn translucent and the sauce thickens (about 15 minutes).
  7. Once the balls are set on the outside, gently stir with a wooden spoon without squashing them.
  8. Taste for salt, add effirin, cook for 2 minutes, and your Ikokore is ready.
